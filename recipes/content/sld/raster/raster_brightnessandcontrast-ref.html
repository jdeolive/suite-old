Brightness and Contrast

Normalize the color output and then increase the brightness by a factor of 2

This example is similar to the Two-color gradient, save for the addition of the <ContrastEnhancement> tag on lines 9-12. Line 10 normalizes the output by increasing the contrast to its maximum extent. Line 11 then adjusts the brightness by a factor of 0.5. Since values less than 1 make the output brighter, a value of 0.5 makes the output twice as bright.

As with previous examples, lines 13-16 determine the <ColorMap>, with line 14 setting the lower bound (70) to be colored dark green (#008000) and line 10 setting the upper bound (256) to be colored dark brown (#663333).
